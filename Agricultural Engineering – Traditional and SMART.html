Agricultural Engineering – Traditional and SMART

Agricultural engineering has evolved from traditional methods focused on manual labor and basic machinery to SMART technologies that integrate innovation and automation. Traditionally, engineers designed simple tools, irrigation systems, and mechanical equipment to aid in planting, harvesting, and soil management. While effective, these methods often relied heavily on human effort and were limited by environmental factors. In contrast, SMART agricultural engineering uses sensors, GPS, AI, and data analytics to create precision systems that monitor crop health, automate equipment, and optimize resource use. This shift not only boosts productivity but also promotes sustainability by reducing waste and improving environmental management. Together, both approaches showcase the evolution of agriculture through engineering ingenuity.
